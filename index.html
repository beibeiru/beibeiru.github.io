About me

I am a postdoctoral research fellow under the supervision of Dr. Peng Jiang at National Cancer Institute (NCI), a component of the National Institutes of Health (NIH). I received the Ph.D. degree in Dr. Jiangwen Zhang Lab from School of Biological Sciences at the University of Hong Kong (HKU), where my research area was cancer systems biology. Prior to joining HKU for my PhD study, I earned a master degree at University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC). My advisor was Prof. Jian Huang and our common research interests were phage display informatics.

Research

PeriodPositionProjects
2019.09 - PresentPostdoc

Integrate spatial transcriptomics and cancer genomics data to discover new knowledge on cancer immunology.

2014.09 - 2019.03 PhD student
  • Construct a comprehensive annotation and visualization database CR2Cancer for chromatin regulators (CRs) involved in cancers through high-throughput data analysis and literature mining. By this database, users can explore the functional, genomic, transcriptomic, proteomic, clinical, biological network and literature-reported information for CRs across cancer types.
  • Identify aberrant CRs affected by genomic alterations across eight cancer types from TCGA through integrating multi-dimensional omics data. Master regulator analysis was further applied to prioritize these aberrant CRs responsible for cancer development. Develop an R package ModReg based on differential connectivity to examine the modulation role of CRs in transcriptional regulation during tumorigenesis.
  • Streamline the above master regulator analysis pipeline, and design an easy-to-use web server MR4Cancer that can prioritize master regulators driving a cancer-related phenotypic divergence according to the input gene signatures.
  • Design an integrated repository TISIDB for tumor-immune system interactions. TISIDB allows users to interrogate the function of a specific gene in tumor-immune interplay through multiple types of data resources in oncoimmunology including literature, high throughput data sets (i.e. TCGA cohorts, screening and immunotherapy) and public databases.
2009.01 - 2014.06 Master student
  • Build a literature-based database and knowledge resource MimoDB for mimotope data, which are peptide sequences derived from phage display technology.
  • Develop an online tool PhD7Faster that can identify clones propagating faster from the Ph.D.-7 phage display peptide library based on support vector machine.
