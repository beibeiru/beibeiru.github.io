About me 

I am a postdoctoral research fellow under the supervision of Dr. Peng Jiang at National Cancer Institute (NCI), a component of the National Institutes of Health (NIH). I received the Ph.D. degree in Dr. Jiangwen Zhang's Lab from School of Biological Sciences at the University of Hong Kong (HKU), where my research area was cancer systems biology. Prior to joining HKU, I earned a master degree at University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC). My advisor was Prof. Jian Huang and our common research interests were phage display informatics.

Research 

PeriodPositionProjects
2019.10 - PresentPostdoc

Analyze cancer spatial transcriptomics data to discover knowledge on cancer immunology.

2014.09 - 2019.03 Ph.D.
  • Construct a comprehensive annotation and visualization database CR2Cancer for chromatin regulators (CRs) in cancers through high-throughput data analysis and literature mining.
  • Identify aberrant CRs affected by genomic alterations as master regulators across eight cancer types from TCGA through integrating multi-dimensional omics data.
  • Design an easy-to-use web server MR4Cancer that can prioritize master regulators driving a cancer-related phenotypic divergence according to the input gene signatures.
  • Develop an integrated repository TISIDB for tumor-immune system interactions, which includes multiple types of data resources in oncoimmunology including literature, high throughput datasets (i.e. cancer cohorts, screening and immunotherapy) and public databases.
2009.01 - 2014.06 Master
  • Build a literature-based database and knowledge resource MimoDB for mimotope data, which are peptide sequences derived from phage display technology.
  • Develop an online tool PhD7Faster that can identify clones propagating faster from the Ph.D.-7 phage display peptide library based on support vector machine.
